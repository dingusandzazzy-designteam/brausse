# Product

## Register
brand

## Users
Mid-market packaging manufacturers — operations managers, plant directors, and capital equipment buyers at folding carton and corrugated facilities across North America. They arrive researching a major purchase decision. Context: serious, time-pressed, skeptical of marketing. They've seen dozens of industrial sites that look identical. They want proof the machine runs and evidence the company stays.

## Product Purpose
Brausse Group is the exclusive North American dealer for ETERNA by BOBST — heavy-duty packaging machinery for folding carton and corrugated production. The homepage drives qualified equipment inquiries by establishing Brausse as a technically credible, premium industrial partner. Success: a visitor who arrived skeptical leaves certain the ETERNA line belongs on their floor, and contacts the team.

## Brand Personality
Precise, confident, earned. The brand speaks like a senior engineer who's installed 47 machines and doesn't need to oversell. Industrial with a premium edge — not outdated catalog, not startup-soft. The machinery is the protagonist; every design decision exists to make it look consequential.

## Anti-references
- Generic industrial equipment catalog sites: cluttered, spec-first, visually dated, zero editorial ambition
- SaaS landing pages: rounded cards, gradient blobs, floating illustrations, repeating feature grids, glassmorphism
- Corporate stock photography: handshakes, generic warehouse shots, blue-tinted "professional team" imagery
- Orange-as-wallpaper: large orange backgrounds, orange-on-orange, brand color used decoratively at scale
- Editorial-magazine aesthetics misapplied: this is not a magazine, not a lifestyle brand, not a design studio

## Design Principles
1. **The machine earns its frame.** Every layout decision exists to make the machinery look consequential — large, isolated, precisely lit.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** Orange marks the single action worth taking. Nothing else competes for that signal.
3. **Editorial chapters, not component soup.** Each section is a distinct visual world. No repeating card grids. No templated icon-heading-text blocks.
4. **Earned credibility over stated claims.** Real numbers, real locations, real machines. The copy already proved itself — the design doesn't contradict it with visual noise.
5. **Premium without luxury.** This is heavy industry that takes itself seriously. Precise, not precious. Industrial, not decorative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Semantic HTML5. Keyboard-accessible navigation and interactive controls. Reduced-motion support via prefers-reduced-motion media query. Visible focus states. High-contrast text on all backgrounds.
